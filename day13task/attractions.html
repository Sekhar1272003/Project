Must-Visit Places

Top Attractions Across India

Hand-picked by our travel experts — the experiences every visitor must have.

01 UNESCO Monument

Taj Mahal, Agra

The ultimate symbol of eternal love — Emperor Shah Jahan's marble masterpiece built in 1653, glowing pink at sunrise and silver under moonlight.

6am–7pm ₹1,100 4.9 Agra, UP
02 Sacred Site

Golden Temple, Amritsar

Harmandir Sahib — the holiest Sikh shrine, built in 1604 and sheathed in 750 kg of pure gold, reflecting in the sacred Amrit Sarovar pool.

Open 24 hrs Free 4.9 Amritsar, Punjab
03 Nature & Backwaters

Kerala Backwaters

900km of serene waterways, palm-fringed canals and rice paddies in God's Own Country. A houseboat stay here is a bucket-list essential.

Year-round ₹6,500/night 4.8 Alleppey, Kerala
04 Palace

Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

The Palace of Winds — a 5-storey sandstone marvel with 953 latticed windows allowing breezes and views for royal ladies in purdah.

9am–5pm ₹200 4.7 Jaipur, Rajasthan
05 Temple

Meenakshi Temple, Madurai

A 17th-century Dravidian masterpiece with 14 towering gopurams adorned with 33,000 brightly-painted sculptures. A living, breathing temple city.

5am–9:30pm ₹50 4.8 Madurai, TN
06 Hill Station

Leh-Ladakh, J&K

The "Roof of the World" — breathtaking monasteries perched on mountain ridges, electric-blue Pangong Lake and some of the finest trekking on Earth.

May–Sep best ₹500 permit 4.9 Ladakh, J&K

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit India

India's size means the perfect season depends on where you're headed. Here's your quick guide.

Winter Season

October – February

The golden window for most of India. Cool, dry weather makes Rajasthan, Agra, Kerala and Goa perfect. Ideal for wildlife safaris in Ranthambore and Corbett.

Rajasthan Agra Goa Kerala Delhi

Summer & Hill Stations

March – June

Plains get hot but the Himalayas come alive. Shimla, Manali, Darjeeling and Ooty are at their lush best. Perfect for Ladakh trekking season opening in May–June.

Ladakh Manali Shimla Ooty Darjeeling

Monsoon Magic

July – September

The rains transform India into a lush emerald paradise. Kerala's backwaters swell beautifully; Cherrapunji and the Northeast explode with waterfalls. Off-peak prices too.

Kerala Northeast Coorg Munnar

Off The Beaten Path

India's Hidden Gems

Skip the crowds. These lesser-known wonders rival their famous counterparts — minus the queues.

01

Hampi, Karnataka

A surreal UNESCO-listed ruined city — the once-mighty capital of the Vijayanagara Empire spread across a boulder-strewn landscape. Massive temple complexes, royal enclosures and stone chariots await the curious traveller.

UNESCO Heritage Ruins Photography
Karnataka
🟢 Low crowds
02

Ziro Valley, Arunachal Pradesh

A breathtaking highland valley dotted with Apatani tribal villages and pine forests — one of India's most scenically dramatic yet rarely visited destinations. Also home to the beloved Ziro Music Festival every September.

Tribal Culture Nature Festival
Arunachal Pradesh
🟢 Very few tourists
03

Mawlynnong, Meghalaya

Famously voted Asia's cleanest village, Mawlynnong sits amid Khasi hills draped in mist and monsoon greenery. Living root bridges made from century-old tree roots, cascading waterfalls and sky-view balconies make it unforgettable.

Eco Tourism Living Bridges Village
Meghalaya
🟡 Moderate crowds
04

Chettinad, Tamil Nadu

A collection of 75 villages showcasing jaw-dropping palatial mansions built by the wealthy Nattukotai Chettiars in the 18th and 19th centuries. Beyond the architecture, Chettinad cuisine is legendary — one of India's most complex spice traditions.

Heritage Architecture Cuisine Cultural
Tamil Nadu
🟢 Low crowds
